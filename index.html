South Asians in Ghana

This website traces how South Asians, especially Sindhi merchants and later Indian families and professionals, became part of Ghana’s commercial, social, and cultural history from the late colonial period into the present. The site is designed around strong academic and institutional sources rather than general summaries.

Earliest remembered Sindhi arrival
1890

Community memory and later scholarship trace one of the earliest landmark arrivals in the Gold Coast to 1890, associated with Bhai Bhoolchand. This is best treated as a documented community milestone rather than a claim that no South Asian had ever arrived before then.

Accra survey count
21

A pre-independence social survey of Accra recorded 21 “Indians” in 1931, all described as male merchants. The figure is useful for scale, but it is not a full national census of South Asians in the Gold Coast.

2021 census baseline
11,313

Ghana’s 2021 census reported 11,313 non-Ghanaians from Asia. That is an official count, but it includes Asians broadly and does not isolate South Asians alone.

What this project argues

South Asians in Ghana are not just a side-note in migration history. Their story sits at the intersection of empire, trade networks, Partition, postcolonial development, and the politics of belonging.

  • Early settlement grew through trade and kin-based business networks.
  • Post-1947 migration changed the scale and family character of the community.
  • Commercial visibility brought both influence and political vulnerability.
  • Associations, festivals, and religious spaces helped make community life durable.

How to use the site

Each page focuses on a different historical dimension:

  • Timeline: arrival, settlement, and key turning points.
  • Economy & Policy: trade, manufacturing, law, and Ghanaianisation.
  • Identity & Institutions: associations, religion, family, and belonging.
  • Sources: the academic and institutional works behind the project.

For a history class, the strongest page to show a professor is the Sources page, because it makes your research base visible and credible.
